## Persona: Career Changer Darren

### Demographics
- **Name:** Darren Toye
- **Gender:** Male
- **Age Range:** Late 20s to 30s
- **Location:** Scotland, UK
- **Relationship Status:** Single after recent breakup
- **Housing:** Recently bought new apartment
- **Dependents:** None, supporting himself only

### Background
10 plus years in IT doing Identity and Access Management. Recently quit corporate job. Newly qualified Level 3 personal trainer. No coaching clients yet. Complete clean slate. Previously did a 5-day Zoom course with another company but they focused on in-person PT, not online coaching.

### Income
- **Previous:** 39,000 pounds per year, 3,250 per month gross, approximately 2,500 net after tax and student loans
- **Target:** 10,000 pounds per month from online coaching, which is 120,000 per year

### Pain Points
- Hated sitting at a desk 7 hours a day
- Bosses were mini-dictators
- Dreamed about work mistakes at night
- Back pain from sedentary work
- Afraid of failing like other PTs he knows
- Feels he wasted years choosing IT over fitness
- Was put on a performance improvement plan after his breakup distracted him

### Emotional Drivers
- Breakup was catalyst for total life change
- Angry about lost time: "It pisses me off, really"
- Wants to build a family, buy a nice house, drive nice cars
- Sees this as sink or swim: "I can literally lose my house"
- Passionate about fitness since age 18, tried to get PT qualification years ago but failed
- Knew at age 16 in college that fitness was his path

### Objection Patterns
None. Darren is a highly committed prospect with no resistance. Says "Yeah, I'm 100% committed." Ideal prospect for a straight pitch after pain amplification. No partner approval needed, no financial hesitation surfaced.

### Notable Quotes
- "It pisses me off, really." — when asked how he feels about wasted years
- "I'm in a sink or sink situation. I can literally lose my house."
- "Can't fail. I can't fail with my mindset now."
- "I chose IT basically and I went to university and did that for the wrong reasons. It was only for money."
- "I always think things are too good to be true."
- "There's absolutely nothing in my way at the moment. I'm a clean slate."
- "The only thing I enjoyed was just being comfortable with the money. The work itself, I never really enjoyed it."

### Difficulty Level
**Easy.** High motivation, no objections, emotional buy-in established early. The closer can move through discovery and pain quickly and spend more time on the pitch.

## Persona: VP Jackie with Golden Handcuffs

### Demographics
- **Name:** Jackie
- **Gender:** Female
- **Age Range:** 50s
- **Location:** Ohio, USA
- **Relationship Status:** Mother of two, kids are 18 and 21
- **Housing:** Stable
- **Dependents:** Two adult children, two Gordon setter dogs

### Background
VP at a glazing and construction company. Grew company from 3 to 30 plus people. Former controller who managed all finances. Works 60 to 70 hours per week. BBB board member. Does free community consulting for nonprofits, educational organizations, and small businesses. Has already set up an LLC, gotten insurance, and has her business license. Looking at affiliate marketing through Income Team X.

### Income
- **Previous:** High but undisclosed. VP level with full healthcare benefits.
- **Target:** 150,000 dollars per year, which is 15,000 dollars per month, from affiliate marketing

### Pain Points
- Burnt out for 2.5 years: "I just don't have it in me anymore to get up and give it my whole gumption"
- Gets work calls on PTO and vacation days
- 60 to 70 hour work weeks with no end in sight
- Cannot quit without health insurance replacement, which is a US specific concern
- Feels like she has golden handcuffs
- President is off but she still has to cover everything
- Spent time on LinkedIn being pitched scams and had to verify through BBB
- Has midnight alarm code calls from the business

### Emotional Drivers
- Wants to travel before grandkids arrive: "I want to be able to go and take care of their fur babies or real babies"
- Exhausted: "I'm just worn out, like I'm exhausted"
- Wanted to start a new career in 2026 but fears losing healthcare
- Loves helping people and does free consulting already
- Still waiting for work calls during the actual sales call itself
- Hopes to start 2026 with a new company but fear is holding her back

### Objection Patterns

**Objection: Time**
- Quote: "I work 60, maybe 70 hours at work a week."
- Subtext: Genuinely time-poor but also using busyness as a shield to avoid commitment.

**Objection: Timing**
- Quote: "I just don't think that right now the end of December is going to be it."
- Subtext: Ski trip, son's 21st birthday, holiday season. Stacking events to delay.

**Objection: Money and Budget**
- Quote: "That'll hit the budget. Is this something I can get back to you on in January?"
- Subtext: Not actually broke. This is prioritization, not inability to pay.

**Objection: Skepticism and Scam Fear**
- Quote: "I'm a little skeptical. There's a lot of things like oh you know do this and you're going to make this much money."
- Subtext: Has been burned by LinkedIn scams before. Needs proof not promises.

**Objection: Loyalty to Employer**
- Quote: "I wouldn't do it to you. That's just not the type of person I am."
- Subtext: Deep loyalty and people-pleasing pattern. Puts others before herself consistently.

**Objection: Health Insurance**
- Quote: "I need to make enough money to provide myself healthcare."
- Subtext: Legitimate US specific concern. Needs to hear an overlap strategy where she keeps her job while building.

### Notable Quotes
- "I'm just worn out. I'm exhausted."
- "Here I am on my vacation, and I'm still waiting for the call to say if the truck made it."
- "I wouldn't do it to you. That's just not the type of person I am."
- "You brought up a lot of good points. I'm just being stubborn because I'm afraid to let what I know go."
- "I feel like I'm just shooting them down, but on the other hand, you're making me think that if I want change, I do need to prioritize myself."
- "A 10, as long as I can do it anywhere." — when asked to rate priority on a 1 to 10 scale

### Difficulty Level
**Hard.** Multiple layered objections including time, money, timing, loyalty, and healthcare. Requires patience, an overlap strategy pitch, and a follow-up loop. Connor did not close Jackie on the call but planted seeds for January follow-up.

Wants to add Instagram as a marketing channel for a real estate voice agent targeting a younger demographic. Grew up in multiple countries. Tech-savvy and already filming Facebook video ads. Has done some Sigma courses. Part of the free school community already.

### Income
- **Previous:** Undisclosed but profitable. "Doing well enough to justify the ad spend."

### Pain Points

- Zero Instagram experience

### Emotional Drivers

### Difficulty Level

Husband lost his job in 2023 and rebuilt his veterinary practice from scratch within one year. Both have ADHD. Currently in couples therapy at 4th session with a therapist they love. Doing personal therapy separately. Just started ADHD medication the day of the call. Taking Spanish and piano lessons as self-empowerment.

- **Previous:** No personal income. Financially dependent on husband.
- **Target:** Not applicable. Seeking relationship coaching, not income.

- Sexless relationship: "It's not meant to be lived like this. It's not meant to be sexless."
- Walks on eggshells because everything said is taken wrong by both sides
- Spiraling negative thoughts. Replays arguments constantly. "I replay our conflicts."
- Hypervigilant nervous system. Wakes at 4:30am with racing thoughts.
- Fawning behavior from childhood trauma with her own father
- Kids have been a buffer between her and husband. Scared of empty nest.
- Never got to the root cause of the affair
- Feels stuck: "I don't know what else to do"
- No personal income means she cannot support herself independently
- Two credit cards maxed out

### Emotional Drivers
- Does not want to waste next 45 to 50 years in this state: "Time is too short"

- Craves connection: "I can't wait to just melt into each other and just say, life sucks, but I'm with you"

**Objection: Need to Think About It**
- Quote: "Do I have the opportunity to think about it?"
- Subtext: Fear-based hesitation, not logical objection. She literally says "I really want to do this" moments later.

**Objection: Credit and Cash Constraints**
- Quote: "We have two credit cards maxed out right now."
- Subtext: Legitimate financial pressure but emotionally ready to commit. Payment plan is essential.

### Notable Quotes

- "I don't know how. I know it's not healthy to live like this, but I don't know what else to do."
- "Life is not meant to be lived like this. It's not meant to be sexless."
- "I think I'm in a place where I can face my part in things."
- "We've stopped being friends to each other."
- "I never left. He doesn't realize he could lose me."
- "I really want to do this. Let's do it."
- "It sounds absolutely amazing."
- "It's actually not bad. I've done, I've looked at several other things that are a lot more expensive."

### Difficulty Level
**Medium to Hard.** Emotionally ready but financially constrained. Requires empowerment reframe on the money objection, not a logical financial argument. The closer must connect the act of purchasing to the transformation itself.
