# Prospect Dialogue Lines for Roleplay Training

## Opening Situation Statements
Lines prospects use when first describing why they are on the call. Use these for the discovery phase of roleplay.

### Darren: Career Change Opening
"I've left a corporate job to go into health and fitness, got my level 3 qualification. Technically I've not even started personal training or anything like that."

### Jackie: Work Life Balance Opening
"I'm really looking for a better work-life balance. I grew with this company from three people to now plus 30."

### Matt: Feeling Stuck Opening
"I kind of just want change in my life. I feel like I'm stuck."

### Julie: Relationship Distress Opening
"Our relationship has been in distress for lots of years. I'm a little scared that it might be just slowly dying."

### Samuel: Channel Expansion Opening
"Right now I'm running ads, so I have a number of voice agents. I thought we should do more outreach."

### Laura: Business Pivot Opening
"I have to change my business now to support him. He's got a lot of eyes on him."

## Pain Acknowledgment Lines
Lines prospects say when they acknowledge their pain during the amplification stage. These are breakthrough moments.

### Pain: Anger About Wasted Time
Darren: "It pisses me off, really." — when asked how he feels about wasted years in IT

### Pain: Existential Stakes
Darren: "I'm in a sink or sink. I can literally lose my house." — when asked about consequences of inaction

### Pain: Burnout
Jackie: "I'm just worn out. I'm exhausted." — when describing how she feels about work

### Pain: Contradiction Exposure
Jackie: "Here I am on my vacation, and I'm still waiting for the call to say if the truck made it." — when confronted about work-life balance contradiction

### Pain: Helplessness
Julie: "Day to day, it's awful. I don't know how. I know it's not healthy to live like this, but I don't know what else to do." — when asked how situation affects her day to day

### Pain: Depression Cycle
Matt: "It's been a cycle and it's been really hard to just break out of it. I've gotten into pretty depressed states." — when asked about impact of staying in current situation

### Pain: Parental Loss and Fear
Matt: "I've lost both of my parents. Seeing what happened with my dad who had some money saved up but it just drained." — when sharing deeper personal motivation

### Pain: Relationship Desperation
Julie: "Life is not meant to be lived like this. It's not meant to be sexless." — when describing what she wants to change

### Pain: Friendship Lost
Julie: "We've stopped being friends to each other. We just manage things." — when describing current state of marriage

### Pain: Golden Cage
Darren: "The only thing I enjoyed was just being comfortable with the money. The work itself, I never really enjoyed it." — when asked about corporate job satisfaction

## Commitment and Buy-In Lines
Lines prospects say when they are ready to move forward. These indicate the transition from pain to pitch is working.

### Commitment: Full Declaration
Darren: "Yeah, I'm 100% committed and ready to push forward." — after identity confrontation question

### Commitment: Fired Up
Darren: "Can't fail. I can't fail with my mindset now." — after pain amplification

### Commitment: Close Moment
Julie: "I really want to do this. Let's do it." — close moment after payment plan offer

### Commitment: Priority Scale
Jackie: "A 10, as long as I can do it anywhere." — when asked to rate priority on 1 to 10 scale

### Commitment: Self Reframe
Matt: "It's an investment in myself to be able to improve my life." — self-reframe during close

### Commitment: Social Proof Acceptance
Matt: "Why can't I be one, right, if other people can do it?" — after seeing social proof and testimonials

### Commitment: Guarantee Acceptance
Matt: "In the grand scheme of things, 90 days is nothing." — after 90-day guarantee is mentioned

### Commitment: Discomfort Acceptance
Matt: "I think I'm ready. I also know that to grow and experience change, you have to be uncomfortable." — after angel vs devil metaphor

### Commitment: Belief Shift
Darren: "I believe I can. The market's out there. I've got the passion for it." — when asked if he believes he can make more money in fitness

### Commitment: Clean Slate
Darren: "There's absolutely nothing in my way at the moment. I'm a clean slate." — when asked if anything else is blocking him

## Objection Lines
Lines prospects say when they resist or push back. Categorized by objection type for the AI to match against the Objection Playbook.

### Objection Type: Think About It
- Matt: "I still want to think about things a little bit. Sleep on it."
- Samuel: "It's not a matter of budget or my timeline. I'll just still have to think about it a bit."
- Matt: "But I still didn't think that I would be signing up at this minute."

### Objection Type: Timing
- Jackie: "I just don't think that right now the end of December is going to be it."
- Jackie: "I think I need to wait, because this would just be way overwhelming."

### Objection Type: Time
- Jackie: "I don't want to commit unless I know that I'm going to get extra time."
- Jackie: "I work 60, maybe 70 hours at work a week."

### Objection Type: Skepticism
- Jackie: "I'm a little skeptical right now, not of you, but of the whole, you know."
- Darren: "I always think things are too good to be true."

### Objection Type: Partner Approval
- Julie: "I'm not the one making the money. So I'm trying to decide, do I go to Jared with this?"

### Objection Type: Money
- Julie: "We have two credit cards maxed out right now."
- Matt: "I need to put money into the house relatively soon. More than this."
- Jackie: "That'll hit the budget. Is this something I can get back to you on in January?"

### Objection Type: Due Diligence
- Samuel: "I have to do my due diligence, if I say it that way."
- Samuel: "That's about it. It's just see what else is out there."

### Objection Type: Risk Aversion
- Jackie: "I can't leave a job that's paying me what they pay me plus my healthcare without having another job to swing to."

### Objection Type: Need Proof
- Matt: "I kind of need to sometimes see it to believe it."

### Objection Type: Process Comparison
- Matt: "Even when I purchased the house, I still thought about it. I needed to do my due diligence and wrap my head around the whole situation."

### Objection Type: Loyalty
- Jackie: "I wouldn't do it to you. That's just not the type of person I am."

## Breakthrough Moments
Lines where prospects shift from resistance to openness. These are the emotional turning points in a call.

### Breakthrough: Self Awareness
Jackie: "You brought up a lot of good points. I'm just being stubborn because I'm afraid to let what I know go. But it's scary." — moment of self-awareness after closer pushes on priorities

### Breakthrough: Clarity
Jackie: "You made a lot of things clearer to me. I think you made a lot of things clearer." — end of call reflection

### Breakthrough: Self Confrontation
Jackie: "I feel like I'm just shooting them down, but on the other hand, you're making me think that if I want change, I do need to prioritize myself." — self-confrontation during objection handling

### Breakthrough: Refusal to Settle
Matt: "I'm not willing to put up with it for any longer." — after being asked if he would settle for the current outcome

### Breakthrough: Support System Proof
Matt: "I've seen that with a good support system in the beginning, things are possible." — connecting house purchase experience to mentorship decision

### Breakthrough: Admission
Darren: "Yeah, I already knew. I just didn't take action, basically." — admitting he knew IT was not right but stayed anyway

### Breakthrough: Ambition Shift
Darren: "I want more now, I want more." — shift from comfortable to ambitious
